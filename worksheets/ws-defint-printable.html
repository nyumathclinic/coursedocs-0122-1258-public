    2.

    Which of the following are equal to \(\displaystyle \int_1^5 \frac{\ln x}{x} \, dx \text{?}\)
    ðŸ”—
    • \(\sum_{i = 1}^5 \frac{\ln x_i}{x_i} \Delta x\text{,}\) where \(\Delta x = 4/n\) and \(x_i = 1 + i \Delta x_i\)
      ðŸ”—
    • This is \(R_5\) approximating \(\int_1^5 \frac{\ln x}{x} \, dx\)
    • \(\lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{i = 1}^5 \frac{\ln x_i}{x_i} \Delta x\text{,}\) where \(\Delta x = 4/n\) and \(x_i = 1 + i \Delta x_i\)
      ðŸ”—
    • The sum counts only the first five terms in \(R_n\text{.}\)
    • \(\displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{i = 1}^n \frac{\ln x_i}{x_i} \Delta x\text{,}\) where \(\Delta x = 4/n\) and \(x_i = 1 + i \Delta x_i\)
      ðŸ”—
    • Yes, this is \(\lim_{n\to\infty} R_n\text{,}\) which converges to \(\int_1^5 \frac{\ln x}{x}\,dx\text{.}\)
    • \((\ln 5)^2/2\)
      ðŸ”—
    • Yes. \(\frac{d}{dx}\frac{1}{2}\left(\ln x\right)^2 = \ln(x) \cdot \frac{1}{x}\text{,}\) so
      \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int_1^5 \int_1^5 \frac{\ln x}{x}\,dx &= \left.\frac{1}{2}\left(\ln x\right)^2\right|^5_1 \\ &= \frac{1}{2}\left(\ln 5\right)^2 - \frac{1}{2}\left(\ln 1\right)^2 \\ &= \frac{1}{2}\left(\ln 5\right)^2 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
    • \(\displaystyle \frac{1}{5^2} - \frac{1}{1^2}\)
      ðŸ”—
    • No, this is not correct. You may be using \(\frac{1}{x^2}\) as an antiderivative of \(\frac{\ln x}{x}\text{,}\) which it is not.
    • \(\displaystyle \frac{\ln 1}{1} + \frac{\ln 2}{2} + \frac{\ln 3}{3} + \frac{\ln 4}{4}\)
      ðŸ”—
    • No, this is not correct. This is the sum of the first four terms in \(R_n\text{,}\) which approximates \(\int_1^5 \frac{\ln x}{x} \, dx\text{.}\) The correct answer is the limit of \(R_n\) as \(n\to\infty\text{.}\)
