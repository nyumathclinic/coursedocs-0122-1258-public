    Section Problem Set 1

    This problem set consists not only of problems similar to what you’ve seen, but also of unique problems you may not have seen before. The purpose of the latter is for you to apply the concepts you’ve previously learned to new, unfamiliar, and usually more interesting situations. In some cases, problems connect ideas from multiple learning objectives.
    🔗
    Write full, clear solutions to the problems below. It is important that the logic of how you solved these problems is clear. Although the final answer is important, being able to convey you understand the underlying concepts is more important.
    🔗
    Prepare a single PDF and submit it to Gradescope.
    🔗

    Exercises Exercises

    1.

    Avery is measuring the volume of water in their backyard on the day of a rainstorm.
    1. Let \(T(h)\) be the total amount of rainwater in Avery’s backyard, in cubic feet, \(h\) hours after 12:00 a.m.
      🔗
      🔗
    2. Let \(A(h)\) be the rate at which the rain adds water to Avery’s backyard, in cubic feet per hour, \(h\) hours after 12:00 a.m.
      🔗
      🔗
    3. Let \(M(h)\) be the rate at which water drains or evaporates from Avery’s backyard, in cubic feet per hour, \(h\) hours after 12:00 a.m.
      🔗
      🔗
    The functions \(T(h)\text{,}\) \(A(h)\text{,}\) and \(M(h)\) are all differentiable functions. Assume there is no water in Avery’s backyard at 12:00 a.m.
    🔗
    (a)
    Write a sentence in English which explains the equality
    \begin{equation*} \int_4^{10} M(h)\,dh = 8000 \end{equation*}
    .
    🔗
    🔗
    (b)
    Write an integral expression which gives the total amount of rainwater in cubic feet, in Avery’s backyard at 7:00 a.m.
    🔗
    🔗
    (c)
    Write an expression which gives the average amount of rainwater, in cubic feet, in Avery’s backyard between 6:00 a.m. and 9:00 a.m.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    2.

    Estimate the area under the curve \(f(x) = \frac{1}{x}\) from \(x=1\) to \(x=5\) using four rectangles.
    🔗
    (a)
    Use right endpoints. Sketch the graph and the rectangles.
    🔗
    🔗
    (b)
    Repeat the estimation using left endpoints.
    🔗
    🔗
    (c)
    Explain why one estimate is an overestimate and the other is an underestimate.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    3.

    Let \(g(x) = \int_0^{x^2} \sin(t^3) \, dt\text{.}\) Find the exact value of \(g'(x)\) .
    🔗
    🔗

    4.

    A particle’s velocity is given by \(v(t) = 3t^2 - 12t + 9\) (in meters/second) for \(0 \le t \le 4\text{.}\)
    🔗
    (a)
    Find the displacement of the particle over the interval.
    🔗
    🔗
    (b)
    Find the total distance traveled by the particle over the interval.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    5.

    Evaluate the definite integral \(\int_{e}^{e^4} \frac{\sqrt{\ln x}}{x} \, dx\) using an appropriate substitution. You must show the change in the limits of integration.
